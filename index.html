Timeline of Important East Palo Alto Events

1783
Belinda Royalls Case
Belinda Royall, formerly enslaved in the Royall House, initiated one of the first cases seeking reparations for slavery in the United States.
1933
Creation of the Home Owners’ Loan Corporation
The creation of this federal agency intended to address the foreclosure crisis that came about during the Great Depression.
1949
Annexation of Belle Haven
Belle Haven, a previously unincorporated neighborhood, was annexed to Menlo Park in 1949.
1954
William Bailey and his family become the first African American family to move into Palo Alto Gardens
Bailey and his family moved into a subdivision in the area called Palo Alto Gardens, and their move triggered white residents to protest their arrival.
1958
Expansion of Bayshore Highway
The expansion of the Bayshore Highway effectively eliminated the downtown of East Palo Alto.
1958
Opening of Ravenswood High School
Ravenswood High School opened with 629 students.
1966
Opening of Nairobi Day School
This school was created to address the inequities in the education system for African American students in the East Palo Alto community.
1969
Opening of the Nairobi College
Nairobi College was opened as an alternative Black educational institution.
1970
Establishment of Shule Ya Taifa
Shule Ya Taifa was one of the first independent African-centered schools established in East Palo Alto.
1976
Ravenswood shuts down
Financial difficulty led to shut down
1976
Filing of Tinsley v. Palo Alto Unified School District Local
Margaret Tinsley filed a lawsuit against the Palo Alto Unified School District.
Establishment of Dr. Omowale's Community Development Institute
Dr. Omowale's Community Development Institute was established to further community development.
1978
Proposition 13 Passed
Amendeded the state constitution to cap property taxes at 1% of assessed value at time of last sale.
1979
Closing of Nairobi College
1983
Incorporation of East Palo Alto
East Palo Alto was incorporated into San Mateo County.
1986
Settlement of the Tinsley v. Palo Alto Lawsuit Local
The settlement led to the creation of the Tinsley Voluntary Transfer Program.
1989
Introduction of the Commission to Study Reparation Proposals for African Americans Act
The legislation outlined a brief history of the injustices brought upon African Americans.
1990
EPA Unethical and Violent Police Department
East Palo Alto police department faced significant issues with unethical and violent behavior.
1990
EPA Community Center Policing
Faith leaders worked to change policing in the city.
1991
Palo Alto, Menlo Park, and the Crescent Part Neighborhood Association suing
City of Palo Alto sued to preveent a project on the border of East Palo Alto.
1992
Community activist and small-business owners suit filing, and Adobe System pulls out
People in East Palo Alto filed suit to block de Monet development.
1995
Ravenswood Highschool Demolition
1996
Establishment of Shule Mandela Academy
Founded as an extension of the educational and cultural mission initiated by Shule Ya Taifa
1998
Proposal to demolish Whiskey Gulch to build the Four Seasons Hotel and 451,000 square feet of class-A office space
An official proposal was pitched to demolish Whiskey Gulch,
1998
The City Council of East Palo Alto met to consider a proposal to demolish Whiskey Gulch
The city council considered a decade-long proposal to demolish Whiskey Gulch
1998
Collard Greens Festival
The Collard Greens Festival was a way to raise funds for the Shule Mandela Academy
2000
The East Palo Alto Police Department switches towards a community-based policing strategy.
2004
Facebook
2009
The U.S. House Resolution 194 and Senate Concurrent Resolution 26
Admitted that African Americans continue to suffer from effects of slavery
2020
California enacted a new law to create a task force to determine how the state could provide reparations to Black Americans and who would be eligible.
Task Force to study and develop reparation proposals.
2021
Amazon Leaves University Circle
Amazon Web Services, a large tenant, leaves University Circle.
2023
Publishing of the California Reparations Report
California Legislature recieved final report from Reparations Task Force.
2023
H.R.40 - Commission to Study and Develop Reparation Proposals for African Americans Act was introduced.
Seeks to establish a commission tasked with studying the effects of slavery.
2024
East Palo Alto Community Archive seeks to create a museum and permanent space
Museum created to maintain memorable information about East Palo Alto.
